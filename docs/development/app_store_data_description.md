Fluster is first and foremost an academic note taking tool, as the original version was built for my own personal use in the pursuit of a model of quantum gravity after leaving my career as a software engineer to pursue this model full time.

Fluster is then my attempt to bring the best tools and workflows I had over my time pursuing this model to the general user, without requiring that they learn to code.

To accomplish this I built Conundrum, a superset of markdown and a subset of mdx (a favorite tool among developers). Conundrum is powered by Rust, making it fast, and it's built specifically for you... the user that never intends on becoming a full-time software engineer. With Conundrum you write your content in the way that suites you best, and the compiler writes your output to a variety of platforms and targets. Even the Fluster blog is written in Conundrum!

This cross-compilation is part of what makes Conundrum so enticing in the age of AI as the Conundrum compiler automatically reformats your notes to the form that AI handles best on the fly, producing the best possible results from a given model.

Fluster isn't just Conundrum content though, you can also store your hand-drawn sketches right alongside your Conundrum based content so you can take full advantage of the searchability that Fluster offers, while still writing in the form that is most comfortable to you!

To help you organize the often chaotic work of a modern academic Fluster also includes it's own bibliography manager with an embedded Biblatex editor, along with support for tags, topics, subjects, dictionary entries and more, making sure that you can always find that one thing you're sure you wrote down 'somewhere'.

I hope you'll take a look at Fluster. I built this over the course of 5 years while pursuing a modified model of quantum gravity, and I'm excited to release both the application and the model together. Please keep in mind that while I'm proud of the current state of the application, I built this while homeless after quitting my job to pursue this model, so there were some design decisions that I had to make that were less than ideal. As soon as those limitations are removed, the capabilities of Fluster will grow exponentially.
